About Us

Heroes for Heroes aims to clear up the intricacy that exists betweens donors and recipients and establishes a clear and user-friendly interface that brings the organizations and people closer together. The purpose of our website is to allow the general public to donate money to buy food for frontline workers. The donations are distributed among our suppliers and every couple of days the orders are acummulated and sent out to the organizations that were specified. Being a non-profit organization, we do not take any cuts from the donations made, but rather ensure that every single penny donated is being used to help the ones who've been helping us. Scroll down to see more information, or press the button below to go straight to donating. Thank you and stay safe! Donate Now

Frontline Workers

Unlike most of us who have the luxury of working from home and good safety from the virus, there are certain people out there who have to walk out in the face of danger everyday so that they can help others. We are referring to the frontline workers, the ones who can't work from home because their jobs require that they serve us. They don't have extra powers to fend off the virus, so the amount of risk that they have been taking is astronomical. It's been quite a while since they've been bravely doing this, so it's time that we show them some appreciation and love by helping them out.
Frontliners include:
  • Doctors
  • Nurses
  • Department Store Employees
  • Grocery Personnel
  • Police Officers
  • Emergency Personnel

How to Help

  • Each marker represents a location that is accepting donations
  • Click on the location that you want to donate to
  • When you click 'Donate Now' you will see two icons pop up. Nothing will pop up if the location bar is empty.
  • Click on the money icon if you wish to donate cash, and the food icon if you would like to donate food
  • Clicking on money will bring out the payment form, and clicking on food will open up the catalog of food that you can choose to order and donate.

Making the Best Of It

Opportunity

Covid-19 has definitely had a big impact on our lives, but if we look at the situation from a positive angle, we can see that we have more time on our hands now. Even though it is important to relax, which you should definitely do more of during this time, it is also important to make good use of your extra time. Some things you can do:

  • Develop your hobbies
  • Learn a new skill
  • Review your academic work
  • If a rising senior, start thinking about college apps

Additionally, make sure to keep some time for yourself and catch up with friends and family.

  • Call and text friends
  • Exercise daily
  • Catch up on your favorite show or discover a new one

Education

Even though school closures might bring upon the thought of no homework, there isn't any good excuse for not learning if you still can. Knowledge can't hurt you, so do take it upon yourselves to revisit school subjects from this year, or visit educational websites such as khanacademy.org. A great place to build upon skills and academically-oriented hobbies is udemy.com, where you can learn anything from coding to social media marketing. Additionally, if you are looking for help on any subjects, Big Brain Tutoring, a local and free tutoring service run by Westwood High School students, is a great service to look at. More information about them can be found at bigbraintutoring.net.*

Stay Home & Stay Safe

It is important that during this difficult time, we all stay home to prevent the disease from spreading. We have to prove that we as humans have enough will-power and determination to stop the disease from taking us out, and the only way we can do that is we follow proper safety guidelines. We are fortunate enough to have the internet to keep us all connected, so let us use that to our advantage and discover things we never had a chance to.

* Big Brain Tutoring has canceled sessions from May 11 - 22 due to AP Exams and from May 25 - June 5, the number of sessions per day will be limited, but from June 6 onwards, the number of sessions per day will return to normal.
